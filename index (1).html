One Dock. The Whole Bay.

Tampa Bay Is Bigger Than You Think.

From Port 32 Marina on Hula Bay, you've got downtown Tampa one way, St. Pete the other, and miles of shallow flats and sandbars in between. Family days, fishing trips, sunset cruises — same dock, totally different days.

Downtown & Harbour Island

Dockside Dining, Bay-Side Skyline

Run north into Hillsborough Bay and tie up at Jackson's Bistro on Harbour Island, or cruise the channel past the downtown Tampa skyline. The city looks different from the water — and the parking is always free.

The Flats & Sandbars

Shallow Water. Big Fish.

Tampa Bay is one of the best inshore fisheries in the Gulf — snook, redfish, trout, and tarpon in water you can almost stand in. Our flats boats are built for it. Or skip the rod, anchor at a sandbar, and let the day do what it wants.

Hula Bay & The Pier

Walk Off the Dock, Into the Day

Our dock sits next to Hula Bay Club and Duke's Retired Surfer's Island Bar — you can be on the water or at a beachside table in under a minute. Cross the Bay for the St. Pete pier and you've got a whole second city to explore.

Local Knowledge Matters

The Bay Rewards Local Knowledge.

Tampa Bay is shallow, beautiful, and full of details that matter — tide changes, channel markers, sandbars that move, and crab traps that don't. New to the area? You'll get an honest read on where to go and what to avoid before you ever leave the dock.

Our team checks weather, tides, and Bay conditions every morning. Members get a quick briefing on what the Bay is doing that day — best fishing windows, where the family-friendly anchorages are calm, and which channels are running where.

Want a captain? They're available on request. Just say the word.
